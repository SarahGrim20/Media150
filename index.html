Sarah Grimsted Lab 6

My name is Sarah Grimsted and I am a visual storyteller. My primary mediums are journalistic writing and painting and I am expanding to include film and animation. What I aim to do with my work is display complexity and feeling through any tools at my disposal. A typical project for me would be a portrait. When I paint a portrait, I begin by trying to map out the sitter's physical self as accurately as possible. I want the audience to get a real sense of the person. Then I make unexpected color and shape choices to exagerate what already exists. A succsesful portrait to me has bright colors and a noticable facial expression.

I carry the portrait-painting process into my writing by focusing on human-centered stories and interviewing. An ideal work of writing for me would help the audience learn from another person, but also tackle some kind of social issue people may be confused about. I've covered community health care workers, college students who feel isolated by social media, young voters supporting Mamdani, and Rock music analyses.

This year, I hope to paint portraits that have conceptual elements and surprise people, cover how immigration policies and censorship effect college professors, code my own adventure, and write some imagery-based poems.
